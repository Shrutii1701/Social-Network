"Salem’s Lot by Stephen King (1975)"

Salem’s Lot, is one of the earliest books written by Stephen King. 'Salem's Lot is a 1975 horror novel by American author Stephen King. It was his second published novel. The story involves a writer named Ben Mears who returns to the town of Jerusalem's Lot (or 'Salem's Lot for short) in Maine, where he lived from the age of five through nine, only to discover that the residents are becoming vampires. The town is revisited in the short stories "Jerusalem's Lot" and "One for the Road", both from King's story collection Night Shift (1978). The novel was nominated for the World Fantasy Award in 1976[1] and the Locus Award for the All-Time Best Fantasy Novel in 1987.

This story of a small town overrun by vampires is deliciously chilling. As residents experience a growing list of strange encounters and start putting the pieces together, you’ll feel yourself tensing up in anticipation of the final reveal and confrontation.

Salem's Lot contains themes that are fairly standard for a book of this genre. Readers should not have to work too hard to see the presence of the supernatural theme, as the book deals with a vampire who is bent on creating a bunch of other vampires in a small town in Maine. Beyond the vampire angle is the fact that a character like Father Callahan exists in the book. He is a character that absolutely believes in a supernatural higher power. This is a different vein of what is “beyond” humanity. On the one hand, there is the supernatural monster element that defies the understanding of humans.
